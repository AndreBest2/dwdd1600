Who Were the Signers

Famous signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of independence include

John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress and the first signer of the Declaration, known for his bold signature.
Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence and third President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
A respected statesman, inventor, and diplomat; he helped secure French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Adams
A key advocate for independence, later became the second President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
A leader in the movement for American independence and a founder of the Sons of Liberty.
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign all four major American founding documents, including the Declaration and the Constitution.
Robert R. Livingston
One of the Committee of Five who drafted the Declaration, though he was not present to sign it.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer of the Declaration and one of the wealthiest men in America at the time.
Richard Henry Lee
Proposed the resolution for independence to the Continental Congress in June 1776.
George Wythe
A prominent Virginian lawyer and mentor to Thomas Jefferson, known for his role in shaping legal education in America.

These individuals were among the 56 delegates who risked their lives by signing this historic document, marking the colonies' decision to break away from British rule.
